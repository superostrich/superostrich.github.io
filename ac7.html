Ace Combat 7 Modding Guides

Simple Texture Replacement Guide

This guide covers extracting textures from AC7, loading and saving them in an image editor, and packing your mod for use in-game. This method uses an extremely simple tool-set comprised of portable apps and python scripts. If you're just getting started, this is the guide for you.

Texture Replacement Guide using UE4 Editor by Dantofu

This advanced guide covers texture replacement using Unreal Engine 4. The process is more involved, but allows for preserving the lowest mipmap level and allows you to use high quality BC7 texture compression. Recommended for modders who have a firm grasp on how texture replacement works.

Blender Quick Start

This guide covers busing Blender to preview & edit your textures on 3D models, as well as ripping models using Ninjaripper. Regardless of which guide you use for texture replacement, you'll want to get familiar with using Blender to assist you in texture editing and previewing your edits.

Normal MREC/Splitting Guide by Dantofu

This advanced guide covers busing Unreal Engine 4 & HxD to create instance files that allow each skin slot to have it's own unique MREC texture and Normal Map. If you're an experienced modder who is feeling limited by MREC and Normal sharing, this guide will open up a ton of possibilities.

Resources

Codename Vanilla

Community made repository of blank, layered, diffuse texture templates for AC7 aircraft.

AC7 Mod Tool

Tool by lotsbiss that allows for opening PAK files, replacing the content within, and re-packaging. Guide coming soon.

IGCS-GITC (Freecam Mod)

Free-Camera mod by ghostinthecamera. Indespensible tool for taking screenshots, or closely inspecting your textures in-engine. The tool allows you to freely move the camera around, change depth of field, freeze time, remove the HUD, and enable supersampling; all on-the-fly.

Useful Links

  • Tailor Made Decals - An online store that specializes in high quality model decals. An excellent reference for small details on aircraft like maintenance, warning, and rescue placards.
  • 1999.co.jp - This online hobby shop frequently includes scans of the instructions for the aircraft models they sell; these make excellent paint-guides for recreaing liveries.
  • Prime Portal - Scale modeller resource site, featuring many walkaround photos galleries of military aircraft.
  • Net-Maquettes - A massive collection of walkaround galleries of aircraft from all over the world on static display, and in museums.
  • Mariusz Wojciechowski's Fulcrum and Flanker pages. Comprehensive catalog of liveries worn by Fulcrums and Flankers, featuring full-color 3-view diagrams.
  • MIL-STD-2161C - Department of Defense manual covering paint schemes & exterior markings for US Navy and Marine Corps aircraft. Ever wonder what all those yellow & red markings & symbols on western planes are? Wonder no more.
